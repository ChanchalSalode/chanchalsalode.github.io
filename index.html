Chanchal Kumar Salode

I’m a fourth-year PhD student at Indian Institute of Technology Delhi (IIT Delhi, India), advised by Prof. Prasanna R. My research lies at the intersection of operations research, optimization, and artificial intelligence. I specialize in developing large-scale mathematical models (Mixed-Integer Linear/Nonlinear Programming, MILP/MINLP) and AI-driven optimization algorithms for complex infrastructure and logistics systems. My current focus is on scalable decision-intelligence frameworks that integrate machine learning with optimization to support sustainable planning in transportation, logistics, and infrastructure networks.

Before starting my PhD, I worked as a Research Associate at Indian Institute of Management Indore (IIM Indore, India), under the supervision of Prof. Saurabh Chandra.

My long-term research vision is to build AI-driven decision-optimization systems that enable sustainable and intelligent infrastructure planning at a global scale.

Research Interests

I’m interested in combinatorial optimization, large-scale optimization algorithms, and artificial intelligence. My current research focuses on developing and solving large-scale infrastructure planning and network design problems through AI-driven mathematical modeling (MILP and MINLP) and intelligent optimization techniques that integrate machine learning with exact algorithms.

  • • Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence for Optimization
  • • Integer / Mixed-Integer Programming
  • • Combinatorial Optimization
  • • Large-Scale Optimization (Exact Methods and Heuristics)
  • • Multi-Objective Optimization
  • • Optimization under Data Uncertainty (Stochastic and Robust)
